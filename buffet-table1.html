Before You Buy

There are a few factors that can influence your choice of sideboard buffet tables, and some practical things that you may want to consider before you purchase your new dining sideboards. Here are some things to think about when buying furniture buffets.

  • Budget: First and most importantly, consider your budget. Unless you are preparing the spread of food yourself, you already need to invest in a caterer, plus you will need decoration. Buffets tables will need to be factored into the event budget, so you know the amount of funds that are available before investing in decorative buffet tables.
  • Guest List: Second, consider the size of the guest list. Naturally, more guests mean that you will need more food, which will definitely impact your decision as to the size, features, and style of your new buffets tables.
  • Event Frequency: If you don’t have an event currently scheduled, but are purchasing sideboard buffet tables in anticipation of future events, calculate the average number of guests that usually show up at your parties.
  • Event Nature: Finally, consider the nature of your typical event. Are you investing in white buffet tables for a one-time wedding reception? Or, does your family traditionally have small get-togethers every Saturday? Are you renting a space to host the event? Or, is it being held in your own kitchen?

It’s important to ask yourself these questions, so you have an idea about how much use you will get out of your sideboard buffet tables, and get a clearer picture of the best type of buffets table to suit your lifestyle.

Features to Consider

While you can definitely just go out and purchase a couple of basic, long, white buffet tables, dining sideboards are available with features that may come in handy depending on the frequency, size, and types of events that you generally host.

  • Storage Options: For instance, you can get a drawer sideboard, cabinets buffets, and sideboards with storage, all of which may come in handy if you want a convenient place to store utensils and condiments.
  • Versatility: These are also good features to look for if you don’t host events very often. This way, you will still get some use out of your dining sideboards in between get-togethers.
