Room Dividers

Wall room dividers have long played a role in interior design. In the distant past, these items were essential privacy screens, while the queens and kings of England sought them out as a statement of style or even status.

Today, room dividers have become decorative elements that span both form and function. Young people living in small apartments — especially studios — often use them to create definition in their space, while others place a room divider screen in their living room or bedroom as a showpiece that helps to define their overall interior design style.

The options for room partitions are endless. If you are on the hunt for one for your home — or if you’re tired of relying on dividing rooms with curtains, here is everything you need to know about room dividers, so you can select the best one for your needs.

Before You Buy

If you are looking for a room divider for your home — whether it’s a traditional two-story modern loft or another type of home — there are some essential questions you will need to have answered.

Size

The great thing about room dividers is they work for any space. However, there are still options you must consider. If you have a home with especially low or tall ceilings, think about the height of the room divider that will work best.

Installation

For the most part, you will likely use a fold room divider that can be set up standing on the floor. However, there are other options you may want to consider, including sliding dividers for your rooms or a hang room divider. Whether or not these options are right for you will come down to where and how you plan to use them.

Design

Room dividers come in all materials and styles. You should think about what works best and where you will place it. Do you want a wood folding screen room divider to go with the rustic design of a space, or are you looking for a screen for the bedroom that will add color, pattern, or texture to the space where you will spend a lot of your time?

You can find room dividers made out of everything from rice paper to wood to faux leather. It all comes down to what is right for your home.

Cost

Like with all home furniture and accessories, there is a wide range of what you can spend. You will probably spend at least $50 on a free-standing one. Keep in mind, however, that the price of these pieces will increase based on the construction and size, with larger-paneled options costing more than smaller-paneled models. At the end of the day, however, you can find a room divider that fits your budget.
