Tables

Tables serve a variety of functions and purposes in a home. They can act as accent furniture, and be placed in positions where they can be noticed and appreciated. You can put vases, pictures, and other items on them that will make your home feel warm and cozy to live in.

Before You Buy

You must first ensure that the console table you purchase is right for the space in which it is to sit. The console table you put in your home will reflect your sense of style and taste. You should verify that the size and shape are well suited to the space. And you should also make sure that the color, materials, patterns, and design suit your aesthetic palate. All this must be done before you buy.

Features to Consider

Console tables come in many different forms, and you will have a range of styles and features to choose from. Here are some you should consider:

Traditional

Traditional console tables are usually made of wood. The latter tends to have a dark finish with rich ornamentation and elaborate decorations. If you are into console tables with moldings, turned spindles, cabriole legs, intricate brass drawer pulls, and other highly refined features, then you should go with a traditional table.

Transitional

These tables contain a mixture of traditional and contemporary decorative elements. They are, however, softer in appearance with decoration that is more pared down. Transitional console tables typically include classical lines and rounded corners. They are known for being both beautiful and highly functional.

Contemporary

Contemporary console tables are more linear in line and overall form. They also have softer and simpler ornamentation. Their designs are uncluttered, and they tend to have light wood finishes. Art Deco, urban, and retro are a few of the sub-styles in this category.

Modern

There is no ornamentation in modern console tables. They tend to have geometric or asymmetrical designs that reflect the latest ideas and materials. Such tables are lean and spare, sleek and polished. They are offered in a variety of materials, including glass, metal, and stone.

Country

As the name suggests, the features of this console table grew out of rustic living. Many such tables mimic the styles that were popular in Colonial America. These tables have lighter finishes and contain little or no ornamentation. They are usually constructed of wood and have scalloped edges, wainscoting, and turned legs.

Eclectic

These tables have become more and more popular over the years. They consist of a blend of different styles and aesthetic ideas. Eclectic tables reflect more the approach and philosophy of the individual designer than any larger design movement.

Materials

You should also pay close attention to the materials that the console table is made of. Here are some of the most common options:

Wood

Console tables made of real wood are enormously popular. These tables are crafted from the timber cut from the trunks of trees. Beech, oak, and maple are among the most commonly used.

Metal

Metal console tables can be made from iron, steel, aluminium, and other alloys. In such a table, only the frame will be metal. The top will be made of glass or some other material. Metal console tables are stylish, durable, and easy to maintain.

Stone

This is another popular material for a console table. It can be made of marble, granite, or slate. Some people opt for fake stone tables. These are engineered from plywood or MDF and only have the appearance of stone. They weigh less and are not as costly.
